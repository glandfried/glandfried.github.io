As a Social Anthropologist, finishing a PhD in Computer Science, I consider the social sciences as an other empirical science, subject to the same commitment to understand, explain and predict for the general welfare. With some colleagues, we are working to lay the foundations of an interdisciplinary social sciences research center in Latin America. The construction of explanatory models of social phenomena is a central goal of our proposal and our interest, which drives us to search for methodologies, new or old, that enhance the results of our research and developments. We only hope that our work will benefit communitarian historical projects that prioritize life over profitability.

Lines of Work

  • Science as an intercultural project of intersubjective agreements
  • Bayesian inference as the way to compute honest beliefs
  • Culture as transgenerational information, a product of a non-ergodic system
  • Patriarchal-colonial-modernity as a destructive force of adapted knowledge
  • Polycentric governance as the only means for ecological re-adaptation

Empathy is the cognitive skill that allowed the development of Homo sapiens: mutual understanding, imitation, language and finally culture. Intellectual honesty is a generalization of empathy on which the (ideal of) scientific development is based, a principle common to the entire human species. Only recently have it been discovered which rules allow as to compute honest-beliefs distributions, ensuring consistent and optimal reasoning in contexts of uncertainty given the available information. While these formalisms are a useful tool for the intercultural project of intersubjective agreements, it is the transgenerational information produced by societies (culture) that is the only one ecologically adapted. The current ecological crisis is preceded by an era of genocide and cultural loss, and is being aggravated by the advance of state-private institutions of the patriarchal-colonial-modern front over local autonomies, preventing communities from developing their own institutions. In a world of uprooted individuals, a pedagogy of cruelty manages to introduce the typical psychopathic structure of our times based on instrumental desire: nature as a thing, people as things. Systems that are out of equilibrium, such as the ecological one, offer a physical advantage for cooperation. Rescuing domestic policy, its negotiation, representation and management styles, a knowledge accumulated by women in their condition as a differentiated group of the species, is essential to face the ecological crisis.
